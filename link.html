Link Light Rail

The Link is Seattle's public light rail service which runs from Sea-Tac airport through Downtown Seattle and recently has grown to include stops to the Capitol Hill neighborhood and the University of Washington. Starting with a creative brief, Hannah and I maintained an equal role and worked closely together on every decision and deliverable of the process. Deliverables included an identity manual (with logo, colors, voice & tone), trains wrap, print assets (business cards, tickets, brochures, ads, etc.), web assets (website, mobile app), as well as wayfinding system, icons, and signage.

The Creative Brief

In order to establish a clear, cohesive and effective strategy, we needed to fully understand the company. This involved pooling research, experiences, and user insights to get a clear sense of important points like the company's background, it's audience, how it is perceived, what sets it apart from alternative choices, it's positioning in the marketplace, the selling features of it's service, and more. A detailed creative brief was established in order to guide our process throughout designing the identity. Most importantly, the brand positioning, brand character, and company promise was determined:

Brand Positioning

For commuters, Link Light Rail is the transit solution that provides an affordable, pleasant experience. Our brand is chosen because it is the only reliable transit option that operates outside the flow of traffic and improves the flow of your day.

Brand Character

We are dependable, approachable, and forward thinking.

Company Promise

We believe as Seattle grows we need a reliable solution to keep the city moving. That's why we bring you accessible transit for today and continually improve to meet the needs of tomorrow.

The Logo

With a creative brief, brand positioning/character/promise, and tonal points to guide our process, we set out sketching over a hundred logo options before narrowing down and then moving to refining iterations the chosen direction.

The final primary brandmark combines geometry and fluidity to represent to the "L" initial of the company name. It conveys a classic, established feel while maintaining a fresh, forward-moving tone.

The logotype is adapted from Gotham Bold. It's slightly rounded corners transmit an approachable feel and its boldness provides support and dependability.

Brand Elements

Blue was chosen as trustworthy and reliable component to the brand. It also represents the rich abundance of water that is a part of the Pacific Northwest. Two distinct shades of blue are used as primary colors to portray the brand. The first is a bright Link Blue which is used in brandmark. The second is a dark Pacific Navy used for the logotype. Additionally there are three secondary colors which are used to support the brand: a Warm Red, Chartreuse Yellow, and Northwest Gray.

Brand Assets and Touchpoints

After establishing the main components and strategy of the brand, Hannah and I built out the rest of the assets. A cohesive, dependable, approachable, and forward-thinking Link Light Rail is expressed in every touchpoint of the brand, representing the company's culture, core values, and heritage.
